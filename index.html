Why it's important to understand we don't control everything.

by Asutosh Variar

The Problem

Creators at the top might tell you that all it took to get there was hard work. However, they often ignore the vast amounts of luck it took to get there. (For reference, you watch 0.0009% of content made every day.)

By believing that we can control everything about our life, we may worsen our mental and physical health for no gain. We're also more likely to fall for the half-truths "gurus" tell us.

Only 4% of creators make a livable wage on their content.

Surely more than 4% work hard?

How do you profit most in a gold rush?

You sell shovels.

Please consider the source. If someone makes the claim that you (and only you) are responsible for your failures, they often have something to sell you.

This also gives them an easy way out, because they can just say you didn't try hard enough.

Survivorship Bias

A logical error that occurs when only the successful outcomes of a selection process are considered.

Why Does This Matter?

Survivorship bias is more powerful than ever. With near-infinite content, the 1% looks like the 50%. This distortion makes us feel inadequate when we're actually doing fine.

The best antidote? Real people. Talk to friends. Connect locally. Progress is never linear, and it's much easier to believe that when you're not comparing yourself to algorithmic highlights.

In a culture that sells effortless success, embrace the normality of the life you lead.
